Thoughts On My All-Time Favorite Films

Blue Velvet

Director: David Lynch
Year: 1986
Genre: Drama/Thriller

"Blue Velvet" is an incredibly powerful film. The acting, setting, score, and imagery all provide a one of a kind viewing experience that not many other films can match. "Blue Velvet" is a favorite because upon first viewing, I did not think much of it. However, hours after the credits rolled, images from the film kept reappearing in my mind, and I felt an urge to watch it again. Once watching it again, the film clicked. The reason why "Blue Velvet" is a film that infects your mind. Every image, character, and line is so essential in creating the dark and twisted world that Lynch explores.

Evil Dead 2: Dead By Dawn

Director: Sam Raimi
Year: 1987
Genre: Horror/Comedy

Sam Raimi seemed to use "Evil Dead 2" as a way to make "The Evil Dead" the way he originally intended to. The original film had a famously low budget, but grew a cult following, giving Raimi enough recognition to create "Evil Dead 2" with much more funding. Bruce Campbell returns as Ash Williams, transforming into the quippy action hero most people are familiar with when discussing the character. Evil Dead 2 is a favorite, because as a viewer, it broke all the rules of what a film could be. Part of me was repulsed by the extreme gore and another part of me wanted to laugh at how absurd the action was. I admired the extremely energetic camerawork and vivid colors. This film showed me that a film does not need to fit into one genre, but rather live in its own world, with its own logic.
