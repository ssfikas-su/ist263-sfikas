Army of Darkness: Film Review

Army of Darkness, directed by Sam Raimi, is the finale in the cult classic "Evil Dead" trilogy. The famous horror trilogy, however, ends with a lighthearted and comedic action/adventure film rather than the low-budget campy horror flicks that preceded it. For this reason, Army of Darkness has little reason to be as successful as it is. It is a complete departure from everything that makes the first two films so famous, and yet it furthers the lore and the character of Ash, giving them the end they deserve. Packed with slapstick comedy and one-liners, Army of Darkness is a fun watch for sure.

David Lynch: My Favorite Director

If you have ever heard something referred to as "Lynchian", chances are it's very weird. David Lynch's work thrives in the world of weird. Lynch has mastered the art of surrealist cinema, beginning his career with the cult classic horror film "Eraserhead", and creating a worldwide phenomenon with his sensational TV series "Twin Peaks", Lynch has cemented himself as a master of cinema and his films have had a profound impact on me throughout the years. Every time I watch a Lynch film, I am in awe of the incredibly imagery and the disturbingly relevant themes consistent in all of his work.
